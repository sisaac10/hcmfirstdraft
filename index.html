Solution: Build a Wireless
Community Internet for Students
Why build one?

  • Affordable
    The network can be built upon current infrastruture and designed to specific needs.
    Consumer-grade equipment can be maintained by volunteers.

  • Kid-Friendly, Distraction Free
    Designed for educational use, the network will filter content to promote a safe and
    productive learning environment.

  • Scalable
    The technology creates a long-term solution for students which can later
    be expanded to connect other residents.
    • Goals

    By October 1st:
  • Supernode and hubs installed at elementary schools
  • Outreach started to families
  • Home installations started

    • Long-term
  • Continued free access for students to connect to educational resources
  • Expand network to connect the general population (through residential installs)
    • Network Components
    Supernode

    Fiber-connected backhaul for students to connect to the global internet and providing fast speeds. Sector antennas send signals to hubs and homes.

    1-2 needed
    $5,000-10,000
    + monthly fiber lease
    School Hubs

    Wireless omni-directional antennas installed on the city's nine PK-8 public schools. Serves as a central access point for students.

    ~6 antennas per hub
    $2,000 each
    Last-Mile

    Small antennas installed on residential rooftops and/or telephone poles which give students connectivity to learn at home. User can self-install.

    1-2 per node
    $100-200 each

    Build a wireless community network backbone to create a long-term solution to the Digital Divide
